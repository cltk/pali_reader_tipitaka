PALI  TRANSLATION CHEAT SHEET:

 

General Case Descriptions:

 

Nominative: Agent/subject and used when no "to be" verb is present ex. Buddho Samano

Accusative:  Direct object/patient/takes direct action

Genative:  Denotes ownership or possession

Locative:  Denotes location such as "at" or "in" and sometimes “in reference to”

Vocative:  Encyclitic/used in quotation: "Monks!, etc.

Ablative:  Used to express the point from which action begins

Instrumental:  (can be agent when verb is past) Expresses the instrument by means of which the action is done

Dative:  The purpose for which an action is done and the person to whom something is given (indirect object).

A Few Verb Form Descriptions:

 

Aorist (irregular common): past tense: ex: did, watched, ate, saw

 

avoca=to say (irregular)

ahosi= was (irregular)

 

Conditional: (rare, but used when an impossible hypothesis in the speaker's position is given) Uses future stem with aorist ending

Example: if it were, if they were, if you were, etc:

 

abhavissa/abhavissaa, abhavissa.msu

abhavissa, abhavissatha

abhavissa.m, abhavissaama

 

 

Future Passive Participle: to be done, to be seen

Ex: kamitabba= to be walked, pucchitabba= to be asked

 

Gerund:

Means "having done something"/describes an action preceding the action of the main statement, and can take a patient in the accusative. 

 

Future:

Same as present endings but preceeded by iss or ess: ex: bhavati becomes bhavissati

 

Causitive (present): To cause to do action: ex. he causes to be born: janeti or Bhaaveti= to cause to be.

 

Optative Tense (present): Should, would, could, etc.

 

Past Participle:

Add ta or ita to end of root.  If used as sole verb in past it agrees in nom. with agent (if active) or patient (if passive).  Ex. Eva.m me suta.m

 

Present participle:

Means something that happens at the same time as the main action

Formed using present stem and declined two different ways:

 

Form 1 endings singular and plural:

 

Nom: a.m, antaa

Acc: anta.m, ante

Ins: ataa, antehi

 

Form 2 endings singular and plural:

 

Nom: maano, maanaa

Acc: maana.m, maane

Ins: maanena, maanehi

Compounds: The distinctions between the various types of compounds are still not completely clear to me.

 

Tappurisa(case compounds):

first word helps define the second in this way:

madhouse--> house of the mad

Notice that it is not that the house is mad, but that it is "for the mad" or "of the mad."

If the house itself were mad, then this would be a kammadhaaraya compound.

 

Bahubbihi compounds(attributive compounds):

Two nouns become something greater than what they are apart

ex: ni=free from

ta.nhaa= craving

nitta.nho= he who is free from craving

 

Dvandas compounds (copulative compounds):

Moon+sun=moonsun and means moon and sun

Ex. Namarupa= mind and body

 

Avyayibhava compounds(adverbial compounds):

Used as an adverb

Indeclinable

Add a prefix that clarifies the noun

Ex: in, out , beyond, after, according to, etc.

Ex. In (adhi) + a woman (itthi)= in a woman= adhitthi

 

 

Kammadhaaraya compounds (adjectival compounds):

 

Young+man= young man
